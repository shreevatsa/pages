The bitmap specified by DVI

Knuth developed TeX and Metafont so that he could produce his books in a format suitable for output on a digital typesetter. The goal was to specify completely where ink would go on the page: the bitmap of black pixels for that output device. In addition to quality, one of his primary goals was stability: that the books should look the same, regardless of changes in technology.

So I was curious to see exactly what the "Knuth-specified" bitmap for given TeX input would be, for a given output device. Surprisingly, this turns out not to be common knowledge in the community of TeX/LaTeX users: most of them do not share Knuth's goals of producing future-proof output for a digital typesetter, so almost everyone uses outline fonts, leaving the rasterization to their PDF viewer or printer driver.

With the help of dvi2bitmap, here are extracts from the bitmap for the same input, for different output devices.
